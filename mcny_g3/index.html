Future City Lab @ The Museum of the City of New York

Future City Lab gives visitors the opportunity to interact with New York City current issues, to imagine the cityâ€™s future by designing a street, a building, and a park, and to discover ways of addressing the challenges, drawing ideas from cities around the world.

My role was to develop the software for 12 activity tables, 12 where visitors create their vision for 12 diffrent neighborhoods in NYC, and the Streetscape Wall - a wall of 4x4 screens showing the visitors' completed designs.

In the press

  • Wall Street Journal
  • The New York Times
  • Gothamist

Visitors learn about the challenges of the city by making design choices that affects different performance meters such as the affordability of your housing project, the mobility level of your street and the biodiversity of your park. They can try to optimize these meters, or completely ignore them and just make something that looks really cool.

When your design is displayed on the wall, you can stand in front of it and see yourself embedded within your own city-scape.

Development was done in C++ using openFrameworks.
